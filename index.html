Project Ideas

‘Cinema Core’ is a streaming service platform targeted towards immunocompromised patients in hospitals across Australia to be able to rent the latest cinematic featured films at their own comfort without risking their health. Similar to 'MediCinema' in the UK and the 'Hoyts Corporation in the Royal Children’s Hospital', however, both of these cases have installed cinemas directly into the respective hospitals. Cinema Core’s main objective is to be a streaming service platform, easily accessible through an app and website to showcase the latest films for patients in Australia on their own technological devices.

According to the Australian Institute of Health and Welfare, in the year 2019 to 2020 (inclusive), there were an estimated “11.1 million hospitalisations for admitted patients” across public and private hospitals in Australia. This statistic however is a decrease by “2.8%” from previous years, however, prior year-to-year changes indicated “a consistent upward trend”. This could be due to the COVID-19 lockdown restrictions in 2020 impacting the hospital system. Cinema Core would have been useful to those eleven million patients who were situated in hospitals for an extensive amount of time as film can be used as an escape and normality from the difficulties that their conditions bring.

Similar to other streaming service applications such as Netflix and Amazon Prime, Cinema Core will provide users the opportunity to watch films through both a mobile app or an online website. However unlike current streaming services, Cinema core mainly provides the latest cinematic feature films for rent to the target audience who are hospital patients who are restricted from leaving the grounds due to immunocompromised conditions. By teaming up with Australian film exhibition brands such as Village Cinemas or the Hoyts Corporation, Cinema Core would be an extension to these services to specifically target and help patients. Cinema Core will work with different hospitals across Australia to recognise patients thus allowing them to join and freely enjoy the benefits the service has.

As Cinema Core’s partnering with an Australian cinema company, alongside the blockbuster films, Cinema Core will also hold special reshowings of old films during similar times as the cinema companies reshowcase them. Users are allowed to rent these films as well as the latest blockbuster films. These reshowings will allow for a broder film range for patients to use similarly to other streaming services. However, the main attraction of Cinema Core is showcasing the latest blockbuster films in comfort without risking users' health.

Cinema Core will work with hospitals across Australia to verify users' identities to join the service. This is done with the users privacy in mind as every patient has the right to privatise their own medical health to be kept secure and confidential. This is why Cinema Core will work closely with hospitals to ensure this privacy is upheld but also that the service is going towards the right people in need.

Something to look out for are those patients who are still considered immunocompromised but are not situated in a hospital due to different circumstances. These patients also suffer with discomfort and being restricted to their household also cuts them off from a life outside, similar to the patients in the hospital. As Cinema Core is designed for patients, it is important to also recognise those who are not admitted long term into hospitals. By doing so, Cinema Core should have a feature where patients can apply to sign up to Cinema Core and the patients’ most frequent hospital can confirm their status which allows for them to also join Cinema Core.

A potential drawback of this is the renting price of the films. A goal would be to have the same prices as theatres do as Cinema Core would be partnering with an Australian cinema company, however, realistically the distribution companies who negotiate with theatres might raise the price due to more revenue being made through a streaming service website and in turn increase prices to rent the films online in comparison to in person tickets as renting would have a longer time frame to watch the film than physical viewings. Renting can also lead to an increase of pirating the latest blockbuster films illegally thus creating a problem in the film industry.

To create the website multiple systems would be used such as Apache to give the users the relevant information they wanted through files. This will be stored on servers all around Australia to keep up and running. As Cinema Core will work with hospitals to grant patients to sign up to Cinema Core, there will have to be a database to hold this information from patient names, hospital name, hospital location, billing information, rented films, etc. This can be stored in databases such as DynamoDB, NoSQL, Oracle to manage this information.

As Cinema Core will be available on both an app and a website, developing these is a skill that is required. A Progressive Web App (PWA) could be used to develop both as it is a hybrid of a “regular web page” and “mobile app”. In doing so, freelance developer skills would have to be hired in order to create and develop Cinema Core’s app and website. This can be done using programming languages such as Objective-C and Swift for IOS apps or Java and Kotlin for Android apps. Hiring a specialist would be the most beneficial to ensure everything runs smoothly and successfully.

If Cinema Core is successful, this will hopefully give much needed entertainment to the millions of hospitalised patients who are suffering due to their respective conditions. Through Cinema Core, patients can have some normality as they are able to watch the latest films much like the rest of the population and also provide escapism in their lives to hopefully allow enjoyment as they are going through a difficult time. The impact overtime will be hope and optimism for patients as they are able to get their minds distracted and also encourage interactions between others such as fellow patients or friends.
